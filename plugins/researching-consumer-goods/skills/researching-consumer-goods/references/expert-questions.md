# Expert Questions — Domain-Adaptive Question Bank

Ask domain-specific questions during Stage 1 (Requirements Gathering) to understand what the user actually needs. Pick the 3-5 most relevant questions per domain. Skip any already answered in the user's initial request.

---

## Universal Questions (All Domains)

Always ask if not already provided:
1. Where are you located? (country, city if relevant for local stores)
2. What's your budget range? (in local currency)
3. Priority: best quality, best value, or price-quality balance?
4. Any brand preferences or brands to avoid?
5. Buying now or willing to wait for sales?
6. Open to used/refurbished or new only?
7. Do you already have specific items in mind? (if yes → skip Stage 2)

---

## Outdoor Gear (Ski, Hiking, Climbing, Camping)

**Key questions:**
- What activity? (resort skiing, backcountry, hiking, climbing, camping)
- What conditions? (temperature range, wet/dry, altitude)
- What sizes do you need? (clothing size, shoe size, helmet circumference)
- Any safety certifications required? (CE EN 1077 for helmets, UIAA for climbing)
- Layering system: what do you already own? (base/mid/outer layers)

**Review sites:** outdoorgearlab.com, switchbacktravel.com, blisterreview.com, bergfreunde.eu/magazine, 8a.pl/blog

**Size considerations:** Some brands use combined sizes (POC: M/L as single SKU, Sweet Protection: S/M, M/L, L/XL). Always verify the sizing system per brand.

---

## Electronics (TVs, Audio, Computers, Phones)

**Key questions:**
- Room size / viewing distance? (for TVs, monitors)
- Primary use case? (gaming, movies, office work, content creation)
- Key specs that matter most? (refresh rate, resolution, battery life, storage)
- Ecosystem lock-in? (Apple, Google, Samsung, etc.)
- Warranty/support importance? (local warranty vs. import)

**Review sites:** rtings.com, wirecutter.com, techradar.com, notebookcheck.net, gsmarena.com (phones)

**Geo considerations:** Regional model variants (US vs EU), voltage differences, warranty coverage by country.

---

## Footwear (Running, Hiking, Casual, Dress)

**Key questions:**
- Primary use? (road running, trail, hiking, daily wear, formal)
- Foot width? (narrow, standard, wide — affects brand selection)
- Pronation type? (neutral, overpronation, supination — for running)
- Terrain? (paved, gravel, mud, rock, snow)
- Waterproofing needed? (GTX/waterproof membrane vs. breathable)

**Review sites:** runrepeat.com, solereview.com, outdoorgearlab.com, switchbacktravel.com

**Size considerations:** Sizing varies significantly between brands and regions (US, UK, EU). Always note the sizing system. Running shoes typically need 0.5-1 size up from street shoes.

---

## Clothing (Outdoor, Casual, Formal)

**Key questions:**
- Occasion/use? (outdoor sport, daily wear, office, formal events)
- Material preferences? (merino wool, synthetic, cotton, down)
- Care requirements? (machine washable, dry clean, technical wash)
- Sizing system? (EU, US, UK — varies by brand and country)

**Review sites:** wirecutter.com, outdoorgearlab.com, realmenrealstyle.com, putthison.com (formal)

**Geo considerations:** Sizing systems differ dramatically. EU 50 ≠ US M. Always ask which sizing system the user prefers and note conversions.

---

## Home & Furniture

**Key questions:**
- Room dimensions? (length × width × height for furniture fit)
- Style preference? (modern, traditional, Scandinavian, industrial)
- Assembly willingness? (flatpack OK vs. pre-assembled required)
- Material preference? (solid wood, engineered, metal, upholstered)
- Delivery constraints? (elevator access, stairwell width, delivery floor)

**Review sites:** wirecutter.com, apartmenttherapy.com, reviewed.com

**Geo considerations:** Furniture retailers are highly regional. IKEA is global but stock varies by country. Delivery and assembly services differ by market.

---

## Appliances (Kitchen, Laundry, Home)

**Key questions:**
- Available space / dimensions?
- Energy efficiency priority? (A+++ ratings, annual cost)
- Key features? (capacity, noise level, smart home integration)
- Installation constraints? (built-in vs. freestanding, plumbing, electrical)

**Review sites:** wirecutter.com, rtings.com (for washers/dryers), reviewed.com, which.co.uk (UK)

**Geo considerations:** Voltage (110V vs 220V), plug types, energy rating systems differ by region. Some brands/models are market-specific.

---

## Generic / Unlisted Domains

For product categories not listed above, ask about:
1. **Intended use** — What will you use this for?
2. **Key specs** — What features or specifications matter most?
3. **Compatibility** — Does it need to work with anything you already own?
4. **Size/dimensions** — Any physical constraints?
5. **Durability expectations** — How long should it last?

Then search for domain-specific review sites: "{category} reviews best {year}" and "{category} buying guide."
